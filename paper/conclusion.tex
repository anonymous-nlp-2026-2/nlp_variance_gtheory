\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Item difficulty is the largest variance component in LLM evaluation (34--38\% in MC), and the model$\times$item interaction is the second largest (30--36\% in MC, 15--17\% in FF), exceeding all manipulated design factors by an order of magnitude---models disagree on which items are difficult, not in overall ability. Multi-facet designs absorb this interaction: a 3-prompt $\times$ 3-seed protocol achieves $G \geq 0.80$ with 178 items versus 552 single-condition (3.1$\times$ reduction; cross-benchmark: 93--128 items). An exploratory format comparison ($n = 5$) suggests item-related variance concentrates in MC (68--72\%) but disperses in FF (31--41\%), motivating per-format calibration. We recommend adopting multi-facet designs, reporting item-level accuracy, and calibrating budgets per format.
